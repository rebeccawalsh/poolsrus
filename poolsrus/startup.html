Pool Start Up

  • Prior to the Start-Up of your Pool
    Prior to the Start-up, please purchase these items at Pools'R' Us or at your local pool supply store.
    • 8 gallons of Liquic Chlorine
    • 4 Gallons of Muriatic Acid
    • Largest bag you can find of Diatomaceous Earth
    • Diatomaceous Earth Scoop
    • 3" Chlorine Tablets - 1 large bucket (Only if you have an inline chlorine tablet feeder)
  • Filling Instructions
    When filling your pool, follow and pay attention to these special instructions.
    • Don't fill your pool with soft or conditioned water.
    • Please leave a hose out for the plasters to fill the pool with. If possible leave 2 hoses.
    • Warp the end of the 2nd hose with a cloth, as the plasterers will have already done with the 1st hose.
    • Begin the filling pool and spa no sooner than 2 hours after the plasteres have left.
    • Use low pressure at first. When the water has risen a foot or 2, you must slowly increase the water pressure.
    • Once you begin filling the pool or spa, don't stop filling until the water is approximately 1" onto the tile in the spa or 3" onto the tile in the pool.
    • Continue filling the pool even if the plasters have not installed the lights yet.
    • Don't let the water from the spa spill into the partially filled pool.
    • Don't get in the pool or spa while they are filling it up.
    • Don't use the auto-fill to fill the pool. It will not be operable until the start-up has occured.
    • Don't attempt to run the equipment or turn on the pool/spa lights. They will not work until the start-up has occured.
    • All remaining clean-up will be done at the start-up.
  • Liquid Chlorine Information
    • If the water temperature is above 70 degrees, add 1 gallon of chlorine every other day.
    • If the water temperature is below 70 degrees, add 1 gallon of chlorine twice a week.
    • Always add chemicals while the pool equipment is running.
    • Never add any chemicals the the spa.
  • Liquid Swimming Pool Acid Information
    • Check the pool acid level prior to adding any chlorine.
    • Never add more than 1 quart of acid at any one time.
    • Always add chemicals while the pool equipment is running
    • Never add any chemicals the the spa.
