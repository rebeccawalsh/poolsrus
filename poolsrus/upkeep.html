Pool Upkeep

Thoroughly cleaning your pool on a weekly basis during the swimming season will ensure clean and safe swimming water.

For your convenience, Pools 'R' Us offers a monthly and weekly maintenance package that makes it even easier to keep your pool clean and ready for fun! Ask a representative today for more details.

Here are steps we take for a typical Weekly Maintenance session.

  • 1. Skim Off Leaves and Debris
    We use a long-handled leaf skimmer to gather up leaves, insects, and any other debris floating on the surface of the pool. We also try to remove debris before it sinks to the bottom of your pool where it becomes difficult to remove and may create stains.
  • 2. Brush
    We use a brush to remove dirt that has collected on the sides and bottom of your pool. The sediment will be brushed toward the main drain so it wcan be easily vaccummed later.
  • 3. Vacuum
    We will submerge a vacuum head and hose and then hook up the vacuum to the filter.
  • 4. Clean Skimmer(s)
    The skimmer(s) should be cleaned out weekly or more often if necessary. Removing debris will allow the skimmer to operate at maximum efficiency.
  • 5. Check Water Circulation
    Your pool's circulation system includes the skimmer, pump, pump strainer, drains, and filter. The system helps chemicals work effectively and ensure that the water is properly filtered. Run your pump long enough each day to make sure the water is properly filtered. We also ensure that each item is clean and in good condition.
  • 6. Check the Filter
    The 3 most popular types of filters - sand, cartridge, and vertical grid DE - screen out debris and particles fro your pool water. We clean and maintain your filter according to the manufacturere's directions, keeping your pool water clean.
  • 7. Shock
    We test your pool water frequently (daily or weekly depending on use) and add chemicals if necessary, following manufacturers' directions. A regular shock treatment cleanses the water of algae, bacteria, dirt, and any other organic matter that may have entered your pool. We follow the manufacturer's diretions for these shock treatments as well.
